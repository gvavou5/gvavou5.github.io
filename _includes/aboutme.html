About Me

I earned my diploma on Electrical and Computer Engineering from National Technical University of Athens (NTUA). Currently, I am a 3rd year Ph.D. student in Computer Science at Universitat Politècnica de Catalunya (UPC) and Barcelona Supercomputing Center (BSC-CNS). My supervisors are Prof. Marc Casas from BSC-CNS and Dr. Lluc Alvarez from UPC. Moreover, I collaborate with Prof. Daniel A. Jiménez from Texas A&M University, Prof. Boris Grot from University of Edinburgh, and Dr. Vasileios Karakostas from NTUA.

My research interests include computer architecture, machine learning for computer architecture, and high performance computing. Specifically, I conduct research on hardware prediction, developing microarchitectural prediction/prefetching mechanisms that inherently use machine learning algorithms. Currently, I am mainly focused on (i) reducing the address translation overheads in virtual memory systems caused by modern hpc, server and graph processing applications via hardware prefetching, and (ii) improving the cache management and cache utilization via explicit hardware techniques.
