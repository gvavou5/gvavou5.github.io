About Me

I earned my diploma on Electrical and Computer Engineering Department of National Technical University of Athens (NTUA). During my studies, I have completed numerous projects that gave me a solid background in Computer Science and Software Engineering. My diploma thesis was aimed at quantifying the address translation performance bottleneck caused by modern CPU applications, examing the efficacy of state-of-the-art hardware TLB prefething mechanisms and proposing novel TLB prefetching schemes capable of capturing the miss patterns of these applications in the virtual address space.

Currently, I am a 2nd year Ph.D. student in Computer Science at Universitat Politècnica de Catalunya (UPC) and Barcelona Supercomputing Center (BSC-CNS). My supervisors are Dr. Marc Casas Guix from BSC-CNS and Dr. Lluc Alvarez from UPC. Moreover, I collaborate with Prof. Daniel A. Jiménez and Prof. Boris Grot from Texas A&M University and University of Edinburgh, respectively.

I am mostly interested in Computer Architecture. Specifically, I do research on hardware prediction and I am developing hardware prefetching schemes that inherently use artificial intelligence algorithms coupled with software optimizations. Currently, I am doing research on the virtual memory subsystem, using modern HPC, Server and Graph Processing applications.

In addition to doing research, I am keen on cooking, writing, travelling, and playing basketball. You can find in my blog some of my multicultural recipes.
